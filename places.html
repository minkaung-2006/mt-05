Bagan/ Temples of Bagan

Bagan located in the Mandalay Region of Myanmar is a historic city filled with thousands of Buddhist pagodas. Bagan is one of the world's greatest archaeological sites on earth. Your imagination will be captured by its mysterious appearance.

Visitors are treated to a picturesque setting as the temples rise above the canopy of trees. In addition to the temples, tourists can take a cruise from Mandalay to Bagan along the Ayeyarwady River for a fun day trip. Along the route, you can take in the beautiful views of the mountains and other sights.

Shwedagon

The Shwedagon Pagoda, also known as the Golden Temple, is regarded as Myanmar's most sacred temple and symbol of Myanmar. Additionally, it is the most significant Buddhist pilgrimage place in Myanmar. Every day, numerous devotees visit the Shwedagon. They walk around the stupa and make offerings to the Buddha.

There is a shrine with a Buddha image at each corner of the octagonal pagoda (one for each day of the week - Wednesday is split into two). According to Eastern astrology, each temple is associated with a planet and an animal sign. Burmese people make offerings of flowers, candles, and water to the shrine belonging to the day of their birth.

Mergui Archipelago

The Myeik or Mergui Archipelago lies in the Andaman Sea off the coast of southern Myanmar. More than 800 islands make up this region, ranging in size from tiny ones with a few palm trees to larger ones with several hundred square kilometers. The archipelago provides fantastic chances for diving and exploring among amazing coral reefs and aquatic life.

The islands of Myeik Archipelago provide a wide range of panoramic views, sights, and adventurous activities. In addition, you can hike through the tropical valleys and kayak through mangroves and up rivers. These opportunities for exploration are almost limitless. A tremendously diverse range of wildlife can also be found on the islands.

Inle Lake

Inle Lake, the second-largest lake in Myanmar, is situated in western Shan State. The lake is bordered on all sides by magnificent, green mountains, providing a spectacular natural vista. Due to the area's abundance of wildlife and vegetation, Inle Lake is home to several uncommon species.

One of the most popular tourist destinations in Myanmar is Inle Lake because it offers the ideal blend of rural Burmese culture and mesmerizing natural beauty. A visit to Inle lake and explore the beauty of nature is a must when visiting Myanmar.

Golden Rock

Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is one of the top tourist locations in Myanmar, which attracts both Buddhists and others who enjoy sightseeing. The pilgrims, the atmosphere, the smells of incense - all will take you in the authentic Myanmar.

Golden Rock, commonly known as Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is in Kyaiktiyo, a municipality in the Mon State of Myanmar. It offers a breathtaking view of the sky, sea, mountains and pagodas. People believe that it is magical if they can touch the boulder. Therefore, by the time they come here, they attempt to touch it once. However, women don't have the right to touch or approach.

Mount Popa

Bagan is located around 50 kilometers from Mount Popa (Mt. Popa) in Myanmar. It is about 45-minute drive from Nyaung Oo Airport, Bagan. Mt. Popa is an extinct volcano that is estimated to have erupted for the final time, over three hundred and twenty thousand years ago.

It was situated 55km southeast of Bagan. Mt. Popa (Taungkalat) is popularly recognized as an abode of many “Nats” (spirits of ancient ancestors) who dwell in various parts of the mountain. In the old days, it was also referred to as the “Mountain of Spirits”. Along the journey to the top, you will see monkeys waiting for treats. From Bagan, you can take a day journey back to Mt. Popa.

Saddar Cave

Sadder Cave is one of the caves you must see in Hpa An. Taking a motorcycle taxi is the most convenient way to get to Sadder Cave on a Myanmar tour; it is around 20 kilometres from Hpa An center.

One of Kayin State's highlights is a trip to the magnificent Saddar Cave. The limestone karst scenery of Zwegabin and the numerous jagged, mountainous outcrops sprouting abruptly from the paddy surrounding Hpa-An host myriad spectacular caves. Saddar involves a cave temple but also a hike through underground passages to the far side of a mountain and a return by wooden boat. It really is something special.

U Bein Bridge

U Bein Bridge is a crossing that spans the Taungthaman Lake near Amarapura in Myanmar. The 1.2 kilometre (0.75 miles) bridge was built around 1850 and is believed to be the oldest and longest teakwood bridge in the world.

The best time to visit U Bein Bridge is around sunset when the setting sun frames the bridge and the trees that stand in the lake. Early in the morning and late in the evening is the most crowded time with visitors; it provides a popular atmosphere image for photographers. Therefore, most tourists come at sunset. Visitors to Myanmar can take a break on pavilions and seats, while locals can sit and chat with tourists.
