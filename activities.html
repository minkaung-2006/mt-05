Balloon ride over Bagan

Imagine soaring above an ancient city with numerous Buddhist stupas and pagodas everywhere you look. The Bagan hot air balloon is a must-do experience where you can enjoy the stunning overview of Bagan when visiting Myanmar.

Ballooning season in Bagan runs from the beginning of October to the middle of April. Sunrise is when most rides are offered, as the cooler morning temperatures allow balloons to float closer to the ancient stupas for a more detailed glimpse. Daybreak also tends to bring with it more dramatic layers of haze, dreamily blanketing the landscape for a perfect photo opp.

Wear Thanaka

You'll notice that a yellowish paste covering most people's faces as you walk through the streets of Myanmar. Burmese men and women have worn this item, known as thanaka, for more than 2000 years.

Thanaka is made from the bark of a specific tree. Preparing the paste and applying it to the cheeks in circles and other creative patterns is a time-honored ritual in Myanmar.

Thanaka also has a practical use in preventing wrinkles by protecting the skin from the sun. Other parts of the face and body such as the arms are often applied as well. People in Myanmar consider thanaka a symbol of beauty so if you decide to wear it yourself, you'll likely receive lots of compliments.

Make a Pilgrimage to the Golden Rock

A visit to the Golden Rock or Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is considered something of a pilgrimage by the locals. The people of Myanmar travel here from all over the country to see this, one of the most sacred places in Myanmar.

According to the legend, the rock, which is located on a mountain ledge, is balancing on a strand of the Buddha's hair. This is the only thing preventing it from falling. On your way to Golden Rock, the enthusiasm of the locals will rub off on you. As you get closer, you can sense the excitement. Most guests undoubtedly regard this as a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity or experience.

People take family pictures and pilgrims gently apply gold leaf to the rock, a privilege only granted to men, as women are not allowed in the direct proximity of Buddhist monuments..

Visit Mandalay's craft workshop

Mandalay, the last royal capital of Myanmar and today Myanmar's second largest city, has long been considered as the cultural capital of the country. It is home to many craftsmen and women and has many traditional workshops.

It is a delightful experience to visit one (or more) of these workshops and see how people make their amazing works of art by hand. Artisans carved wood and marble; gold leaf traditional puppet making; taping Specializing in pottery and sewing.

It is possible to purchase authentic souvenirs from the workshops you visit.

Explore the temples of Bagan

It is easy to understand why Bagan is one of Myanmar's most visited tourist attractions. It is a remarkable place with a very long history.

From the ninth to the thirteenth centuries, Bagan was the capital of the Pagan Kingdom. More than10,000 Buddhist temples, pagodas and monasteries were built during this period. At its peak, the city was a cosmopolitan centre for religious and secular studies, attracting monks and scholars from India, Sri Lanka and Cambodia.

Bagan is the kind of place where you can easily spend a few days exploring the temples, brushing up on Myanmar's history, and learning about Buddhism. A nice bonus is Bagan's world-famous sunrises and sunsets.

Climb the 777 steps to Mount Popa

Mount Popa, also known as Taung Kalat, is a monastery located on top of an extinct volcano plug. Mount Popa can easily be visited as a half-day trip from Bagan. If you decide to go, you must be willing to climb its 777 steps, surrounded by hundreds of monkeys. It's quite an adventurous and unforgettable experience that will teach you a lot about Burmese culture.

Explore the Inthas' lifestyles

Inle Lake, famous for its floating villages and gardens, is a place where you can explore the extraordinary lives of Inthas. There are several stops you can make on a boat tour of the lake. In the beginning, you get to see the Intha fishermen, who use one leg to balance themselves on the front of their boat, and the other to row.

Along with fishing and floating gardens, many cottage industries such as silversmithing and silk weaving form a significance of the local economy. You'll see lotus weavers who skillfully perform their handicrafts at weaving industries. Silversmiths and blacksmiths also have their workshops on the lake, and you can visit a cheroot factory. All these craftsmen will happily show you, their skills.
